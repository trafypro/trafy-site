“A computer would deserve to be called intelligent if it could deceive a human into believing it was human.” Alan Turing, One of the Founding Fathers of Artificial intelligence.

The idea that Machines will one day think has always fascinated humans. There has also been a constant debate about whether it is good or not. Artificial Intelligence is increasing the pace of human evolution by using machine learning and deep learning architecture that can analyze, learn, and generate valuable responses. These types of AI assistants speed up the process of discovery and innovation.

What is Artificial Intelligence?

As the name denotes, Artificial Intelligence are machines that can perform tasks like humans but without all the cognitive bias through unsupervised learning of accumulated data. Artificial Intelligence uses advanced algorithms to break down and align structured and unstructured data to perform a certain task through deep learning and natural language processing methods.

Evolution of Artificial Intelligence

Surprisingly we human race was able to theorise and create Intelligent Machines within a short span of less than 75 years. We are certain that in the next 75 years, Artificial Intelligence will be highly empowered and we will have solved most of our challenges across all fields.

So where it all started,

In 1950, Alan Mathison Turing an English mathematician, Computer scientist, and the inventor of the Turing Machine - The world's first computer wrote a paper called “Computing Machinery and Intelligence” which paved the way for most science fiction and actual research.

Then in 1956, John McCarthy an American computer scientist and cognitive scientist coined the term “Artificial Intelligence” for what was referred to as thinking machines. He also convinced the Rockefeller Foundation to invest in a summer camp workshop which he called Dartmouth Workshop for 10 renowned scientists to get together and work on this topic.

In 1964, Joseph Weizenbaum a German-American Computer scientist and professor created a chatbot capable of holding conversations with humans.

In 1997, IBM built a supercomputer called Deep Blue that was capable of playing chess. It defeated a famous chess player Garry Kasparov in a complex chess match. Kasparov referred Deep Blue to as an “alien opponent”.

In 1999, Sony Launched the first robot pet dog AiBO which could develop it’s personality and skills over time.

In 2002, An American company iRobot invented and mass-produced an autonomous robotic vacuum cleaner Roomba. Roomba was able to detect obstacles and navigate the floor area of homes.

In 2011, Apple integrated its digital assistant SIRI into iPhones. This was the beginning of natural contact less interaction of humans and machines.

In the same year, IBM’s supercomputer WATSON won the First place in an American Reality TV show called Jeopardy. And so Rise of the Machines happens.

In 2014, Amazon launched its version of a digital assistant called Alexa Intelligent Virtual Assistant that could complete the user's shopping tasks.

And the inception of the Generative AI Era Started when Open AI, an American Artificial Intelligence Research Organisation was founded in the year 2015 by of prominent tech giants.

In 2020, Open AI introduced a tool for automated conversation called GPT-3.

In 2022, ChatGPT was made available to the public.

In 2023, Google launched its own advanced Generative AI tool, Bard.

Already AI's contribution in the fields of automobile, space research, medicine, architecture, agriculture, chemistry, biology, environment, and entertainment is extremely appreciated as it boosts our growth and improves global connectivity.

But everything has its own origin story. We don’t know yet whether AI will be a Hero or Villain but AI scientists, enthusiasts, and contributors warn that it all depends, on whose hand it is.
